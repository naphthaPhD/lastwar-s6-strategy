# S6 Week 3 Defense Policy Draft

## Date

2026-05-27

## Status

Draft. This is a current working policy and may change depending on alliances, actual map positions, each alliance's intention, and enemy movement.

## Context

#534 is entering Week 3 under a worst-case assumption that upper cities and connected defensive lines may collapse. The current operational priority is not ranking-tier optimization, but avoiding a critical collapse by preserving city count, adjacency, and the ability to delay enemy progress.

Recent chat/event logs indicate that several alliances are already discussing temporary occupation, protection refresh, and later handover operations. SHA appears to be functioning more as an adjustment / protection-rotation coordinator than as a fixed city-defense owner. 4tH, MOE, RGWC, n09, kOi/RGWG, and others are involved in protection and handover discussions.

## Core policy

Week 3 should be treated as a defense-establishment week.

Main objectives:

- Avoid reducing total city ownership as much as possible.
- Preserve adjacency around cities.
- Split fishing-ground protection timers between 07:00 and 23:00 windows.
- Secure at least level-1 altars reliably.
- Prepare to support #509's center and upper side if #509 collapses.
- Build or preserve routes toward #476, #503, and #480.
- Delay enemy progress rather than chase a high-tier ranking immediately.

The guiding principle is:

> Do not try to defend everything. Preserve city count and adjacency, and delay enemy progress.

## Protection rotation concept

Use alternating protection windows so that the enemy cannot break through all layers at once.

Pattern A:

| Line | Protection release window | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1st line | 07:00 | Morning recapture / adjustment |
| 2nd line | 23:00 | Main battle-window defense |
| 3rd line | Flexible | Reserve / emergency adjustment |

Pattern B:

| Line | Protection release window | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1st line | 23:00 | Main battle-window defense |
| 2nd line | 07:00 | Morning recapture / adjustment |
| 3rd line | 23:00 | Secondary night defense line |

The point is not to make a beautiful allocation table. The point is to avoid simultaneous exposure.

## Temporary occupation and handover

If an original intended owner cannot occupy or defend a location due to adjacency, timing, or member availability, a nearby alliance may temporarily occupy it to apply protection.

After the protection period, the location can be handed over if the original alliance can connect and occupy it.

This should be considered a legitimate defensive tool, not a permanent ownership decision.

Examples observed in current discussion:

- 4tH temporarily taking locations to prevent #476 from taking them.
- MOE later inheriting locations after protection timing allows.
- RGWC adjusting capture timing to 07:00-10:59.
- kOi/RGWG alliance or handover options being considered.

## Minimum success condition

The minimum goal for Week 3 is:

- Successfully defend or protect-refresh at least one important city/fishing-ground cluster.
- Confirm practical occupation speed and protection timing.
- Establish a repeatable handover/protection-rotation method.
- Avoid a chain collapse caused by lost adjacency.

Even one successful defense cycle is valuable because it gives alliance leaders a concrete operation pattern.
